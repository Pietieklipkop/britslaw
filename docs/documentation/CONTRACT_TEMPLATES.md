## Template 1: Offer to Purchase (Residential Property)

**Type:** Property
**Jurisdiction:** South Africa (all provinces)
**Estimated time:** 15–20 minutes
**Party roles:** Buyer, Seller, Agent (optional)

### Sections (in order)

| #   | Section Title                     | Description                                                    |
| --- | --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | Property Description              | Define the property being sold (address, erf number, extent)   |
| 2   | Purchase Price                    | The agreed sale amount                                         |
| 3   | Deposit                           | Deposit amount, due date, and escrow terms                     |
| 4   | Balance of Purchase Price         | How the balance will be paid (cash / bond)                     |
| 5   | Suspensive Conditions             | Conditions that must be met for the sale to proceed            |
| 6   | Occupation & Possession           | When buyer takes occupation, occupational rental if applicable |
| 7   | Transfer & Registration           | Conveyancing attorney, transfer timeline                       |
| 8   | Electrical Compliance Certificate | Who obtains and pays for the CoC                               |
| 9   | Plumbing / Water Compliance       | Water bylaw compliance                                         |
| 10  | Voetstoets                        | Property sold as-is clause and defects                         |
| 11  | Agent Commission                  | Estate agent commission (if applicable)                        |
| 12  | General Conditions                | Entire agreement, governing law, notices                       |

### Key Clause Alternatives

**Section 4 — Balance of Purchase Price:**

- _Default:_ Bond finance clause (subject to bank approval within 30 days)
- _Alternative A:_ Cash purchase (no finance required — incompatible with default)
- _Alternative B:_ Seller finance / instalment sale

**Section 5 — Suspensive Conditions:**

- _Default:_ Finance condition only
- _Alternative A:_ Finance + sale of another property condition
- _Alternative B:_ No suspensive conditions (unconditional)

**Section 10 — Voetstoets:**

- _Default:_ Standard voetstoets (sold as-is)
- _Alternative A:_ Excluded voetstoets (seller warrants condition)

## Template 5: Shareholders Agreement

**Type:** Corporate
**Jurisdiction:** South Africa (Companies Act 71 of 2008 compliant)
**Estimated time:** 25–35 minutes
**Party roles:** Company, Shareholder (multiple, minimum 2)

### Sections

| #   | Section Title                               |
| --- | ------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | Company Details                             |
| 2   | Share Capital & Shareholding                |
| 3   | Directors & Management                      |
| 4   | Decision Making (quorum, voting)            |
| 5   | Dividend Policy                             |
| 6   | Transfer of Shares — Right of First Refusal |
| 7   | Transfer of Shares — Tag-Along Rights       |
| 8   | Transfer of Shares — Drag-Along Rights      |
| 9   | Non-Compete                                 |
| 10  | Deadlock Resolution                         |
| 11  | Funding & Capital Calls                     |
| 12  | Exit Mechanisms                             |
| 13  | General Conditions                          |

### Key Alternatives

**Section 4 — Decision Making:**

- _Default:_ Simple majority (50%+1) for ordinary resolutions
- _Alternative A:_ Supermajority (75%) for key decisions
- _Alternative B:_ Unanimous consent required for all major decisions

**Section 10 — Deadlock:**

- _Default:_ Mediation first, then arbitration
- _Alternative A:_ Russian roulette clause (one party offers to buy the other out)

**Jurisdiction:** South Africa

**Party roles:** Disclosing Party, Receiving Party

### Sections

| #   | Section Title                          |
| --- | -------------------------------------- |
| 1   | Definition of Confidential Information |
| 2   | Obligations of Receiving Party         |
| 3   | Permitted Disclosures                  |
| 4   | Term of Confidentiality                |
| 5   | Return of Information                  |
| 6   | Remedies                               |
| 7   | General Conditions                     |

### Key Alternatives

**Section 1 — Scope of Confidentiality:**

- _Default:_ Mutual NDA (both parties exchange confidential information)
- _Alternative A:_ One-way NDA (only Disclosing Party shares confidential information)

**Section 4 — Term:**

- _Default:_ 2 years from agreement date
- _Alternative A:_ 5 years
- _Alternative C:_ Indefinite (perpetual confidentiality)

## Notes for Template Authors

1. **All party names must be defined in Party Definitions** before any clause can reference them via merge fields.
2. **Default clauses** should represent the most commonly used / safest option for the typical client.
3. **Alternative clauses** should be ordered from most-to-least common, not by risk level.
4. **Explanations** should be written at a Grade 10 reading level — avoid legal jargon.
5. **Examples** should reference South African names, Rand amounts, and local contexts.
6. **Conflict rules** must be reviewed by the firm's supervising attorney before publishing.
